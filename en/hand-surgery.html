The human hand consists of 27 bones, including 8 bones that make up the wrist joint, 5 other bones that make up the metacarpal bones, and 14 bones called the phalanges that make up the five fingers, and those bones are linked together with a large number of ligaments and muscles with fine functions.

Because of the complex composition of the human hand, and due to the extent of our dependence on the hands in our daily lives in all activities, the slightest pain or dysfunction in the hand causes a major disruption in a person's life, performing functions, and enjoying hobbies.

Surgical hand treatment types

Carpal tunnel syndrome treatment (CTS)

It is a problem in which the median nerve suffers from pressure at the wrist, leading to inflammation and impaired function, and this results in numbness and pain in the arm, hand, and fingers.

Ganglion cyst treatment hand 

Ganglion cysts are small cysts that form in the hand and wrist over the joints and tendons, often above the wrist or in the palm, and are often filled with transparent fluid and sometimes cause some pain.

Trigger finger surgery

It is a condition that affects the tendons of the hand, which are the cords that connect the bones to the muscles and transmit the movement of the joints. These tendons pass through a series of rings or tunnels.

Normally, the movement of the tendons in their tunnels is soft and smooth, but these tunnels become narrowed in this disorder, impeding the movement of the tendon of one or more fingers.

Rheumatoid arthritis symptoms and treatment

It is a type of chronic inflammation that affects the joints of the hand and results in deformation in the hand due to the formation of the scar tissue caused by chronic inflammation, which obstructs the movement of the fingers.

Scaphoid fracture signs and symptoms treatments 

It is one of the eight bones that make up the wrist bones and the most prone to fracture, and what makes it more complicated is the fact that it loses its only artery when broken, making it difficult to heal.
