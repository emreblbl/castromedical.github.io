What is cancer?

Cancer is not a single disease, it is many diseases, each of which has its own characteristics, signs, symptoms, and treatment. The term cancer refers to the out-of-control rapid division of cells due to imbalance in the division mechanisms which lead to the formation of tumor.

In which category of people does cancer appear?

Cancer can appear at any age, in any patient, and any circumstances, However, the risk for developing cancer increases significantly with age.

What factors increase the susceptibility to cancer?

Several factors increase the susceptibility to cancer and they vary from one person to the other, and from one type of cancer to another, for example, exposure to sunlight increases the probability of skin cancer, but it does not cause lung cancer, for example.

Also it has to be mentioned here that just because you have the risk factors to develop a particular type of cancer does not mean you will develop that cancer, as there are many other factors that play into the malignant transformation of cells, some of them are not fully understood.

The most common factors that increase the susceptibility to cancer

Among the most common factors that may play a role in developing any type of cancer:

  • Smoking
  • Poor diet.
  • Alcohol abuse.
  • Exposure to sunlight.
  • Some microbes and viruses.
  • Certain materials such as asbestos.
