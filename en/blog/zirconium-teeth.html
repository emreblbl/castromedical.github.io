Zirconia crown side effects

The drawbacks and disadvantages of zirconia crowns and bridges are minimal compared to their advantages, and here are some of its drawbacks:

  • Zirconia teeth can sometimes be difficult to match with normal teeth.
  • Possible erosion of other teeth. In some circumstances, some dentists are reluctant to use zirconia crowns for fear that zirconia stiffness will cause the corresponding teeth to erode.

Zirconia dental implants problems

  1. The toughness of the material has raised a few concerns about friction against the tooth root and wearing down contradicting teeth.
  2. The material does not do well in low-temperature or humid environments. when exposed for long periods to cold temperatures, the material loses durability. However, there have not been many tests done in terms of the long-term use of these products.
  3. Installing Zirconia implants in substandard locations can negatively affect the material.
  4. High cost compared to other coating types.
  5. Difficulty in detecting tooth decay under the coating or crowns.

Before zirconia crown preparation

  • Tasks are distributed before installation, between the dentist and the patient.
  • For the dentists, they should give special attention to the preparation of the tooth, the preparation of the anterior teeth is different from the posterior ones, and generally, when preparing the tooth for zirconia crown and bridge, make sure that there is enough space for the thickness of the wall to be at a minimum of 0.5 mm, at least between 1 mm and 1.5 mm, or 1.5 to 2 mm to reduce the occlusion.
  • During one visit, a temporary crown can be installed, and the permanent crown can be installed on the next visit.
  • Furthermore, your doctor will treat any infection or inflamed tissue.
  • The area below the tooth should be clean and healthy before installing the crown.
  • The patient's tasks are to keep the teeth clean, temporary dental prosthodontics until the end of treatment, as well as follow instructions.

After zirconia crown installation

  • Zirconia's crown and bridges are known to be long-term, so those interested in this treatment must be fully aware of zircon's characteristics and care methods to preserve it.
  • Zircon crowns are designed to protect teeth from increased damage, so they need to be properly installed, to avoid complications.
  • Zirconia Crown and Bridges errors cannot be ignored but must be treated immediately for patient safety.
  • Doctors should thoroughly examine crown margins to avoid possible errors during installation.
  • If the crown is not properly installed, this allows saliva and bacteria to enter the crown from margins or spaces, and this can lead to tooth decay and infections.

Can zirconia crowns be whitened?

  • Traditional teeth whitening methods do not work on zirconium, porcelain, or other materials used in dentistry, making it impossible to whiten zirconia teeth after installation.
  • Zircon teeth resist spots caused by coffee, tea, and nicotine much better than ceramic or acrylic crowns, which is very important for those who prefer a natural smile and give it the highest priority.
  • Adjustments to the crown color may be necessary over time, as natural teeth change due to staining or aging.

Zirconia crown vs Porcelain crown

  • Although porcelain and metal teeth have some advantages, this type does not provide the same aesthetic results as zirconia, due to its durability and exceptional strength.
  • Zirconium crowns generally last longer than porcelain, while porcelain crowns usually need to be replaced after a few years.
  • Zirconium crowns showed a 99% survival rate after five years.
  • Zirconium crowns are multicolored and very transparent.

Zirconia dental implants vs Titanium

Titanium has been around longer, has more clinical studies and examinations, and decades of evidence proving it as a dependable and successful biomaterial for dental implants. However, Zirconia is emerging as a promising alternative to conventional titanium-based implant systems. In addition, Zirconia has been observed to have superior soft-tissue response, biocompatibility, and esthetics to that of titanium implants.
